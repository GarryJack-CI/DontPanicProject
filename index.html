What is Anxiety?

Anxiety is a normal emotion, it’s our brain’s way of reacting to stress and alerting us when we are in danger. However, when regular, and excessive anxiety and fear overwhelm us to the point where it interferes with our ability to cope with everyday tasks, this could be considered as an anxiety disorder. Anxiety disorders are the most common mental health disorder. There are several types of anxiety disorders, characterized not only by distressing, persistent anxiety but often by the dysfunctional behaviors that reduce that anxiety.
Here are some examples:

General Anxiety Disorder

Having regular or uncontrollable worries about many different things in your everyday life. Typically, these symptoms would be present for at least six months.

Social Anxiety Disorder

Manifests as an overwhelming fear or anxiety in response to social situations, encompassing interactions in various settings such as workplaces, social gatherings, and parties.

Panic Disorders

Can involve experiencing recurring episodes of intense fear or discomfort, commonly known as panic attacks, which occur unexpectedly and without an identifiable trigger.

Phobias

An intense and irrational fear or anxiety response that arises when encountering a specific situation or object, often leading to avoidance behaviors.
